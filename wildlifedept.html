Sri Lanka's Wildlife Conservation Department

The Department of Wildlife Conservation in Sri Lanka, established in 1949, is the government body responsible for the preservation, management, and sustainable use of the country's diverse wildlife. Its main objectives include safeguarding endangered species, managing national parks and reserves, preventing illegal wildlife trade and poaching, and promoting public awareness about wildlife conservation. The Wildlife Conservation Department in Sri Lanka manages a network of protected areas, including national parks, nature reserves, and sanctuaries, to preserve the country's diverse flora and fauna, ensuring their long-term survival and contributing to ecological sustainability and the nation's cultural heritage.
